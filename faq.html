Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about matchplay rankings.

From how ratings are calculated to which matches count, these answers cover the most common questions about the Matchplay Rankings archive.

Which matches are included in the rankings?

Singles matchplay results from the Ryder Cup (since 1927), Presidents Cup (since 1994), and WGC Match Play (since 1999) are included. Foursome and four-ball formats are currently excluded but will be added at a later date.

How are player ratings calculated?

Ratings are calculated using an Elo-style system. Every new player starts at a rating of 1000, and then gains or loses points after each match based on the result and the difference between their rating and their opponent's rating. This ensures that strength of schedule is taken into account. There is also a K factor which increases the amount of points new players can earn, and a decay which means inactive players' ratings slowly drop.

What does “Active” mean in the filters?

Active players are those who have played a matchplay match within the last five years, based on the current year.

Are halved matches treated differently?

Yes. A halved match awards 0.5 points in records and is treated as a draw in the Elo calculations.

Why do some players have fewer matches?

The archive focuses on singles matchplay events only, so players who primarily competed in team formats or in different eras may have fewer recorded matches.

How often is the data updated?

The archive is updated after each new matchplay event. We publish updates as new results become available and verified.

Can I compare two players head-to-head?

Head-to-head comparisons are in progress. For now, use player filters to review individual match lists and records.

Do team points count toward the rankings?

No. The rankings only use individual singles match results to keep comparisons consistent across events.

What if I spot an error?

Please get in touch and include as much detail as possible. We verify every correction before updating the archive.

Where does the data come from?

The dataset is compiled from official event results and curated to align names, rounds, and scores across competitions. A range of sources from Wikipedia to the PGA Tour to the Ryder Cup are used. They have been compiled with the help of AI tools but always sense checked by a Human before being published here.
