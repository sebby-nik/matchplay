Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about matchplay rankings.

From how ratings are calculated to what matches count, these answers cover the most common questions about the Matchplay Rankings archive.

FAQs

What matches are included in the rankings?

Singles matchplay results from the Ryder Cup, Presidents Cup, and WGC Match Play are included. Team and four-ball formats are excluded.

How are player ratings calculated?

Ratings are calculated using an Elo-style system. Every player starts at 1000, then gains or loses points after each match based on the opponent’s rating and the result.

What does “Active” mean in the filters?

Active players are those who have played a match within the last five years, based on the current year.

Are halved matches treated differently?

Yes. A halved match awards 0.5 points in records and is treated as a draw in the Elo calculations.

Where does the data come from?

The dataset is compiled from official event results and curated to align names, rounds, and scores across competitions.
